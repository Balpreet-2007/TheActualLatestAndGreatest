The History of Sikhism

Sikhism

Sikhi, more commonly known as Sikhism, is a religion that originated in Punjab, India. It was founded by the first Guru of Sikhism, Guru Nanak Dev Ji, in the late 15th century. Sikhism is a monotheistic religion.

Facts

Symbols of Sikhism

  • Kesh - Uncut hair
  • Kara - Steel bracelet
  • Kirpan - Small sword

About Sikhism
  1. There are ten human Gurus, and the eleventh Guru is the religious text of Sikhism.
  2. Gurudwaras are the places of worship for Sikhs.
  3. Sikhs serve langar, which are free meals offered to anyone at any time.
  4. Sikhs wear paggs (turbans) to cover their uncut hair.
  5. The official language of Sikhism is Gurmukhi.
